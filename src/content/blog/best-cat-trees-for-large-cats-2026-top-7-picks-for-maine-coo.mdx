## Key Features to Look for When Shopping

*Because your fur baby deserves the best.*

**Multi-Level Design with Gradual Progression**
Look for trees that don't require Olympic-level jumping between platforms (your cat's joints will thank you). Large cats benefit from gradual height increases that don't strain those precious bones and muscles.

**Reinforced Connection Points**
The joints where platforms meet posts take the most stress during those enthusiastic landings. Quality trees use metal brackets and reinforced screws, not just wood screws that can strip over time (trust us, we've seen too many wobbly disasters from cheap construction).

**Non-Slip Surfaces**
Carpeted or textured platforms provide better grip for large cats, especially important for breeds like Ragdolls who aren't always the most coordinated (and we love them for it).

## Safety Considerations for Large Cat Trees

*Safe, quality, and loved by pets.*

Safety isn't negotiable when it comes to your gentle giant. Wall anchors should be included and actually used – a 25-pound cat can generate serious force when jumping (physics is real, even for our graceful felines). Check weight ratings carefully, and remember that dynamic loads from jumping cats create much more stress than static weight.

**Warning: Inspect your tree regularly for loose screws, worn sisal, or damaged platforms.** Large cats are harder on furniture than their smaller cousins, so maintenance becomes even more critical for their safety.

## Assembly and Maintenance Tips

*We've tested it so you can trust it.*

**Assembly Reality Check**
Large cat trees are substantial pieces of furniture (we're talking real furniture here, not toys). Plan for 2-3 hours of assembly time and definitely have a helper available. The pieces are heavy, and some steps absolutely require four hands (trust us, we've tried the solo approach – it's not pretty).

**Maintenance Schedule That Works**
Check all connections monthly and retighten as needed. Replace worn sisal rope promptly – large cats can destroy scratching surfaces with impressive speed, and exposed wood can splinter (something no loving pet parent wants to risk).
